What are the best exercises for beginners to start with?

1.Warm-Up: Prioritize warming up your joints and muscles before starting your workout. Begin with bodyweight exercises to prepare your body for more intense movements. Incorporate dynamic movements like running for 5-7 minutes to further warm up the muscles and prevent injuries and muscle soreness.

2.Preparation: Determine your motivation and purpose for going to the gym, and prepare accordingly. Bring essentials such as a water bottle, headphones, and appropriate attire. Consider trying out different gyms before committing to a membership to find the right fit for your needs.

3.Workout Program: Consult with a personal trainer or instructor to develop a basic program that focuses on building a strong foundation. Stick to the program consistently to see progress and avoid overexertion. Or, study and work out.

4.Exercise Form: Prioritize good form during exercises to avoid injuries and effectively target the correct muscle groups. Take the time to learn and practice proper execution for each exercise. Muscle and joint formation.

5.Nutrition: Understand the importance of nutrition in supporting your fitness goals. Focus on building a strong foundation before considering bulking or cutting phases. Keep training sessions to 60 minutes or less to maximize efficiency. Be aware that roughly 70-80% of muscle growth is dependent on nutrition.

6.Essential Gym Equipment: Familiarize yourself with essential gym equipment and learn how to use each machine with proper form and technique. This ensures safe and effective workouts. Learn how to use each machine with proper form and technique.

7.Final Tips: Make training a part of your life.

Bodyweight Squats: Strengthen the lower body; target quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, Glute Bridges, and Lunges. Maintain proper form.

Push-Ups: Compound exercise for chest, shoulders, triceps, and core. Begin with modified versions and progress gradually.

Strengthen Core: Planks, abs, obliques, and lower back. Enhance stability and posture. Start with short holds, increase duration gradually, maintain a straight line from knees to head.

Step-Ups: Beginner-friendly lower body exercise. Target quadriceps, hamstrings, and glutes. Improve balance and coordination. Gradually increase step height.
